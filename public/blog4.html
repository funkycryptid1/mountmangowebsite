    Blog 4: maybe a boring blog

    July 7th, 2026 - About 7:57 PM

    I'm super tired. I got back from doing something I wasn't sure I could do, a spur of a moment thing, which was going to a

    big city on my own and to a big concert. Never been to a concert before. And gosh darn it, it was amazing. It was spectacular

    and breathtaking and amazing and... I said that already right? I'm tired, too. Don't think I said that one.

    I also got some perspective after talking to people I probably won't meet again, but it's usually experiences with strangers you think

    about more becasue you don't get the chance to alter the minsicule moments you share with them, but that doesn't mean they aren't significant.

    In fact, they're probably even more important because of that -- someone gets a brief glimpse into your life and decides to share knowledge of

    their own or leave you alone. I won't go into specific detail because I like reserving some stuff for me to ponder, but I will talk about that

    impact I keep bringing up.

    Aside from the orchestral, electrical sway Tame Impala delievered on a very rainy night in Miami, which put me in awe throughout the

    entire night, accompanied with a to-die-for light show that makes you think - "Hey, maybe life is worth living" -- I met some

    really lovely people. And if it weren't for the rain, my having been in the wrong line two times, I probably -- no almost certainly, wouldn't have

    met those people. I'm starting to practice being more appreciative of where I am rather than wondering where I'm supposed to be, or if where I am

    is the right place or whatever, grass is always greener and such. It's a myth. What isn't a myth is the present moment, something I'm trying to

    realize everyday. Being more confident, more outward, maybe not so much... but just enough to accept the now and just have a good time, especially

    at a freaking concert, omg. Accepting the now also means accepting what you want to change.

    From that epic experience and from looking at how other people chase their dreams, it makes me realize that if I really want this, THIS, being my

    wanting to be a storyteller, I can't passively go for it. If I want the life I know I deserve, I need to fight for it, darn it. I can't let this fear

    of being known, misunderstood, disliked, or anything like that keep me from barreling through and just trying! This blog would be a million pages long

    if I went into depth about every influence that made me come to this very... basic conclusion and write this blog, but know that on the surface level

    it came from being in the moment and lights of the astounding musicians for a night, taking a shot of confidence and talking to the people around me I

    don't know, and looking at how other people I know and don't know are relentlesley going after what they want. I want that. To be more me. Cause it

    felt so good to be somewhere I wanted to be, it felt like a dream, not even real, but it was so AWESOME. Back to level 1 writing, I see.

    Anyway, I'm trying to work on the comic/adverts everyday and I think I've slacked off for like a week now, lol. I'm going to try not just to manifest, but to

    actually put myself more in a big way, more than I've been doing before... somehow. Somehow. My thing is that I don't want to be super into social media

    (more than I am already) by worrying about posting stuff relating to my stories, seeming cringe, blah blah. To heck with it. To be frank, and that's not my name,

    it would probably be more productive to be analytical when it comes to seeing what works and what doesn't when promoting my content, rather than doomscrolling

    for the entire day. So, enough talk! I'm going back to working on Mount Mango. Bye. Thanks for reading --

    M
