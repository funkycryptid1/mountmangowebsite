    Blog 1: a brief, but very cool introduction to the mountain known as mango

    June 6th, 2026 - About 5:42 PM

    Come one, come all... to an unfinished site! I'm very pleased with the progress I made in the past few days because there was a point where I couldn't do very simple things when it came to website building. I've got plans for the site in the making, and boy, am I darn sure I can accomplish them all. I've come to learn if you wanna make it in the website game, there's a lot more to it than making the website run... but for now, that's good for me.

    But enough on that, enough on the ideas of websites and technicalities and what else, you're here to read, right? Read a... creatively drawn comic and engage in such an amazing story, years of the making by the way, that has fun characters, meaning, build up, plot, structure, and everything else you'd come to expect from a good story. Well, go click that "READ" link if you want that, otherwise, sit down and chillax while I talk a bit about the making of it and how I got to this point.

    I like rambling, and so the "Extras" space is the place where I'll do that, with stories relating to the comic, or plain whatever I feel goes here cause it's my site. Ideally, if in the future, I would hope, this section could be more than just my big boring blocks of text, but also *nudge nudge* fanart and questions, too, regarding my beautiful story!

    I've worked on the story of the comic since late 2019 and it was originally supposed to be an RPGMaker game (which I had huge, crazy fleshed-out ideas for which would not have worked out for a first-time game dev). So I decided, "Hey, I can draw, and I still want to tell a story, so let's make it a comic."

    So I did, and jeez, I could have done some better planning in the beginning, but really, the most important part is to start and believe in yourself. I haven't commited to a project as long as this and dang it, I'm proud. These scribbles you scroll past and see are the current crowning achievement of my life. And since 2019/2020, I wasn't consistently working on this comic, I had work and school going on so I didn't build discipline then to consistently work on the comic; the start of that happened literally a few weeks ago. But I am glad to be really close to the point where I can show all of you what I've done for the past .... six years? Man.

    The job now is to... finish this site, promote it accordingly (which I am very much looking forward to finding a fun/effective way to do that) so I have readers at that start line, and links ready to build a freakin' fandom, cause darn it, I want that. I know I said this blog would be about the comic and just turned into me talking about not the comic, but that's fine. I've got blog posts for other ideas that do pertain to the comic, more specifically: how music inspires the comic, my process, themes, worldbuilding, amongst other things you welcome you into my creative habitat.

    Still reading? Great, my attention span isn't as good as yours. I would have zoned out and went doomcrolling on Instagram by now. This is my world, enjoy it! I don't wanna explain so much, since I want to see if people get the themes and messages I'm trying to portray on their own (which will happen only if I did a good job telling the story) especially in the later parts of the story. Esentially, I want to make this story my life, telling it to you, reader I will never know probably, because it's what makes me happy and one of the things that makes life worth living for me (drawing and writing). Without it, I couldn't tell you what I'd be. So I propose a deal, darn it, valued reader. I would like to make storytelling my life, to break me out of a monotonous life. I'd like to sell cool merch from the comic, get a show, blah blah blah, all things aspiring artists want for their properties -- but like I said, it's a deal, and my side of it is to give you a good story, one that's worthy of you giving me what I want.

    So if I don't make good on my deal, I don't get what I want. You get a crappy story, I don't live a cool life and I fall into oblivion. By cool life, I mean just a comfortable one where I could spend my time not making comics or telling stories however I want, (crazy, huh?). But here's the thing, I don't think I have a crappy story. I think I'm finally getting into my element (with chapters 6/7) of telling the story in the way I envisioned. Not to say "NO EVERYTHING ELSE BEFORE IT SUCKS, DON'T READ IT!" No, read all of it, por favor, I worked really hard on it, thank you, and it'll be important for the future. It all is. Everything is important. Everything I've gone through in my life, even if it sucks or it's me making mistakes, or whatever, got me here and now. And here is now isn't so bad. I hope we could make this deal, reader. But that only works if I do good for you. And I think I will.

    Thanks for reading --

    M
